Leadership

  • Technical leadership: The path to the CEO's office should not be through the CFO's office, and it should not be through the marketing department. It needs to be through engineering and design.

Personal Growth

  • Feedback loop: Very important to have a feedback loop, where you're constantly thinking about what you've done and how you could be doing it better.
  • Seek criticism: Really pay attention to negative feedback and solicit it, particularly from friends.
  • Choose extraordinary: It is possible for ordinary people to choose to be extraordinary

Entrepreneurship
â–¼
  • People matter most: Starting and growing a business is as much about the innovation, drive, and determination of the people behind it as the product they sell.
  • Extreme difficulty: Being an entrepreneur is like eating glass and staring into the abyss of death
  • Collective innovation: When somebody has a breakthrough, it is rarely one little thing. Very rarely, is it one little thing. It's usually a whole bunch of things that collectively amount to a huge innovation.
  • Talent and teamwork: Talent is extremely important. It's like a sports team, the team that has the best individual player will often win, but then there's a multiplier from how those players work together and the strategy they employ.
  • Quality over quantity: It is a mistake to hire huge numbers of people to get a complicated job done. Numbers will never compensate for talent in getting the right answer.
